Telecom Data

A Call Detail Record is created whenever a phone call is placed or received from a mobile phone on a given provider’s network. These are also called circuit switching data, as within telecommunications engineering, placing or receiving a call involves a switch of an electronic circuit. These records contain:

- the anonymized user identifier for the customer
- the nationality or home region in Italy of the user’s SIM card
- the date and time of the event
- the coordinates of the tower that handled the call

** CDRs can potentially contain text message (SMS) data as well, although SMS records were not present in our data.

In this project, we analyzed CDR data from the time period between June 1 to September 30 2016. The data contained records for foreign or Italian tourists, covering the span of their visit to the province of Florence as well as 3 days before and after their visit. The Collective Sensing organisation proceeded to removal of Florentine residents from the dataset prior to our analysis of the data, by filtering out people who consistently appeared in Florence during nighttimes over a given period of time. This process was done to protect the anonymity of residents, since call records are sensitive information. Even though the call records are anonymous, the ability to see the nightly travel patterns of users at night and during the day could, in theory, partially allow to identify individuals.

With the help of CS Research, we analyzed the anonymized call activity and behavior in Florence of Vodaphone users over the summer of 2016. The analyses show the number of presences in Florence over the summer of three categories of people: Italian visitors, foreigners, and residents. The values for presences are estimated from the dataset based on multiple factors, such as call behavior trends, local demographics, and cell phone carrier market share. Presence is defined as the number of unique people seen in Florence over 1 day independent of the duration of stay, rather than the number of people present at one point in time (which will be strictly less than the presences). Italian visitors are defined as persons who own SIM cards from Italy and whose home is outside of the city of Florence. Residents are defined as persons who live in the city of Florence. This distinction can be made by looking at the home region associated with the SIM card and from the calling patterns of the customer. Foreigners are defined as persons with foreign SIM cards visiting the city of Florence, with the nationality coming from the nationality of the SIM card. Note that foreigners who buy Italian SIM cards on arrival for use will appear in the data to be Italian.

Total estimated daily presences

The total estimated presences per day for all types of people in the city of Florence during the summer. We can see that on an average day, one third of the people in the city are tourists. That ratio increases in the month of August as foreign tourism remains somewhat constant, whereas the resident population makes a dip. We can also see cyclic activity that is likely due in part to reduced calling on weekends.

Estimated daily presence of foreign visitors

The estimates for foreign presence. The breaks are done by region of the world, with the most common tourist countries (The United States, France, Germany, Spain, and China) plotted separately instead of aggregated into their region. This shows that peak visiting months are not the same for every country.

Estimated daily presence of Italian visitors

The estimates for presence of Italian visitors. We see that the majority of Italian visitors to Florence come from elsewhere in Tuscany.
