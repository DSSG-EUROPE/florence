Figure 1. The total estimated presences per day for all types of people in the city of Florence during the summer. We can see that on an average day, one third of the people in the city are tourists. That ratio increases in the month of August as foreign tourism remains somewhat constant, whereas the resident population makes a dip. We can also see cyclic activity that is likely due in part to reduced calling on weekends.
Figure 2. The breakdown of the estimates for foreign presence. The breaks are done by region of the world, with the most common tourist countries (The United States, France, Germany, Spain, and China) plotted separately instead of aggregated into their region. This shows that peak visiting months are not the same for every country.
Figure 3. The breakdown of the estimates for presence of Italian visitors. We see that the majority of Italian visitors to Florence come from elsewhere in Tuscany.
Figure 4. The breakdown of the estimates for number of visits foreign and Italian visitors make to the city of Florence during the summer months of June 1st, 2016 through September 30th, 2016.
Figure 5. The breakdown of the estimates for how many days foreign and Italian visitors stay in the city of Florence during the summer months of June 1st, 2016 through September 30th, 2016.
